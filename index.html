The
Sixties

1960

March 6

The United States announces that 3,500 American soldiers will be sent to Vietnam.

May 23

Prime Minister of Israel David Ben-Gurion announces that Nazi war criminal Adolf Eichmann has been captured

August 17

The newly named Beatles begin a 48-night residency at the Indra Club in Hamburg, West Germany.

November 8

In a close race, Democratic U. S. Senator John F. Kennedy is elected over Republican Vice President Richard Nixon.

December 20

The NLF(Viet- Cong) forms in South Vietnam, to oppose the government of President Ngo Dinh Diem.

1961

January 17

In his Farewell Address President Eisenhower warns of the increasing power of a "military–industrial complex."

April 17

The Bay of Pigs Invasion of Cuba begins; it fails by April 19.

May 4

U.S. Freedom Riders begin interstate bus rides, to test the new U.S. Supreme Court integration decision.

June 4

John F. Kennedy and Nikita Khrushchev meet during two days in Vienna. They discuss nuclear tests, disarmament and Germany.

August 22

Ida Siekmann jumps from a window in her tenement building trying to flee to the West, becoming the first of at least 138 people to die at the Berlin Wall.

1962

February 5

French President Charles de Gaulle calls for Algeria to be granted independence.

March 19

Bob Dylan's debut album is released in the United States.

May 27

The Centralia mine fire is ignited in Pennsylvania. It is still burning to this day.

July 9

American artist Andy Warhol premieres his Campbell's Soup Cans exhibit in Los Angeles.

August 5

Marilyn Monroe is found dead from an overdose of sleeping pills and chloral hydrate at her home in Brentwood, Los Angeles.

1963

January 14

George Wallace becomes governor of Alabama. In his inaugural speech, he defiantly proclaims "segregation now, segregation tomorrow, and segregation forever!

March 28

Director Alfred Hitchcock's film The Birds is released in the United States.

April 7

Yugoslavia is proclaimed to be a socialist republic, and Josip Broz Tito is named President for Life.

June 11

In Saigon, Buddhist monk Thích Quảng Đức commits self-immolation to protest the oppression of Buddhists by the Ngô Đình Diệm administration.

August 28

Martin Luther King Jr. delivers his "I Have a Dream" speech on the steps of the Lincoln Memorial to an audience of at least 250,000.

1964

January 5

U.S. Senator Barry Goldwater of Arizona announces that he will seek the Republican Party nomination for President.

February 25

Cassius Clay (later Muhammad Ali) beats Sonny Liston in Miami Beach, Florida, and is crowned the heavyweight champion of the world.

March 4

Teamsters President Jimmy Hoffa is convicted by a federal jury of tampering with a federal jury in 1962.

April 20

Nelson Mandela makes his "I Am Prepared to Die" speech at the opening of the Rivonia Trial, a key event for the anti-apartheid movement.

June 17

American author Ken Kesey and his Merry Pranksters embark on their cross-country trip aboard Further (bus) spreading LSD.

1965

February 21

African-American Muslim minister and human rights activist Malcolm X is assassinated in New York City.

March 9

White supremacists beat white Unitarian Universalist minister James J. Reeb in Selma. He later dies.

April 17

The first Students for a Democratic Society march against the Vietnam War draws 25,000 protestors to Washington, D.C.

August 11

The Watts Riots begin in Los Angeles, ending on the 16th after resulting in 34 deaths and over 3,000 arrests.

November 11

In Rhodesia (modern-day Zimbabwe), the white-minority government of Ian Smith unilaterally declares de facto independence.

1966

March 1

The Ba'ath Party takes power in Syria.

March 8

Nelson's Pillar in O'Connell Street, Dublin, is clandestinely blown up by former Irish Republican Army volunteers.

April 21

Ian Brady and Myra Hindley go on trial at Chester Crown Court, for the murders of 3 children who vanished between November 1963 and October 1965.

August 1

Sniper Charles Whitman kills 14 people and wounds 32 from atop the University of Texas at Austin Main Building tower, after earlier killing his wife and mother.

October

Bobby Seale and Huey P. Newton found the Black Panther Party.

1967

January 2

Ronald Reagan, past movie actor and future President of the United States, is inaugurated the new governor of California.

March 12

The Velvet Underground's first album, The Velvet Underground & Nico, is released in the United States.

June 5

Six-Day War begins: Israel launches Operation Focus, an attack on Egyptian Air Force airfields; the allied armies of Egypt, Syria, Iraq, and Jordan invade Israel.

August 25

American Nazi Party leader George Lincoln Rockwell is assassinated in Arlington, Virginia.

October 8

Guerrilla leader Che Guevara and his men are captured in Bolivia; they are executed the following day.

1968

Apri 4

Martin Luther King Jr. is shot dead at the Lorraine Motel in Memphis, Tennessee. Riots erupt in major American cities, lasting for several days afterwards.

Apri 11

U.S. President Lyndon B. Johnson signs the Civil Rights Act of 1968.

June 5

U.S. presidential candidate Robert F. Kennedy is shot at the Ambassador Hotel in Los Angeles. Sirhan Sirhan is arrested. Kennedy dies from his injuries the next day.

November 5

Republican candidate Richard Nixon defeats Hubert Humphery in United States Presidential Election.

December 20

The Zodiac Killer is believed to have shot Betty Lou Jensen and David Faraday on Lake Herman Road, Benicia, San Francisco Bay, California.

1969

April 22

Robin Knox-Johnston becomes the first person to sail around the world solo without stopping.

August 9-10

Members of the Manson Family invade the home of actress Sharon Tate killing her and her friends. They would go on to murder 2 more people the following night.

August 14

British troops are deployed in Northern Ireland to restore order marking the beginning of the 37-year Operation Banner.

September 1

A bloodless coup in Libya ousts King Idris and brings Colonel Muammar Gaddafi to power.

December 1

The first draft lottery in the United States since World War II is held.
